PVC Coated Fabric

PVC, or polyvinyl chloride, is available as a woven or non-woven material and has been a staple in the fabric membrane roofing industry since the early 1960s. PVC membrane is a cost-effective alternative to traditional roofing systems and can be produced in a multitude of colors to coordinate with individual building project needs. When produced in bright white, this flexible fabric membrane reduces radiant heat gain, keeping interior temperatures cooler during warmer weather conditions. PVC membrane is commonly coated with a protective acrylic or PVDF topcoat with a design life of approximately 15-20 years. If PVC membrane is exposed to high levels of UV in such areas like Florida or in the Southwest U.S., the design life of PVC is approximately 10-15 years.

Technically, PVC is a molecule comprised of carbon, hydrogen, and chlorine. The combination of these elements results in a single-ply membrane that is simple to manufacture. During fabrication, remnants can be recycled and melted down to be reproduced, resulting in less waste. Because PVC is a thermoplastic, this membrane can be softened into a semi-solid state and infused with UV light inhibitors and anti-soiling fungicides. During installation, multiple sheets of the fabric can be overlapped and fused together in a process known as weldable seam technology. Once cooled at room temperature, these sheets form one continuous membrane.

Comparison of Acrylic vs. Polyester Fabrics in Layer Functionality

Feature Acrylic Fabric Layers Polyester Fabric Layers
Core Strength Base layer provides moderate strength, suitable for light loads. Base layer is robust, ideal for high-tensile applications.
UV Resistance Coating layer ensures excellent UV resistance and color retention. Coating and topcoat layers offer high UV stability for outdoor use.
Waterproofing Coating layer ensures complete waterproofing. Coating layer plus topcoat prevents water infiltration effectively.
Durability Suitable for medium-term applications in controlled environments. Ideal for long-term outdoor structures exposed to harsh weather.
Ease of Maintenance Protective finish reduces cleaning needs for light applications. Lacquer or PVDF topcoat ensures long-term cleanliness and durability.
Applications Best for awnings, facades, and vertical screens. Ideal for tensile roofs, stadiums, and heavy-duty outdoor setups.
